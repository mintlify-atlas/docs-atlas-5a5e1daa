## Contributing to Shades of Purple

Thank you for considering contributing to Shades of Purple! This theme is used by over 5 million developers worldwide, and your contributions help make it better for everyone.

## Reporting Issues

When reporting issues, please include:

<Accordion title="What to include in your issue report">
  - **VS Code version**: Help > About
  - **Theme version**: Check the Extensions panel
  - **Operating System**: Windows, macOS, or Linux
  - **Screenshots**: Visual issues are easier to debug with screenshots
  - **Steps to reproduce**: Help us understand the problem
  - **Expected vs actual behavior**: What should happen vs what actually happens
</Accordion>

<Note>
  Before opening a new issue, please search [existing issues](https://github.com/ahmadawais/shades-of-purple-vscode/issues) to avoid duplicates.
</Note>

## Code of Conduct

Shades of Purple follows the Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct. We are committed to fostering an open and welcoming environment.

### Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in our project and community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age, body size, disability
- Ethnicity, gender identity and expression
- Level of experience, nationality
- Personal appearance, race, religion
- Sexual identity and orientation

### Our Standards

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Positive Behavior" icon="check">
    - Using welcoming and inclusive language
    - Being respectful of differing viewpoints
    - Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
    - Focusing on what's best for the community
    - Showing empathy towards other members
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Unacceptable Behavior" icon="xmark">

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project team at **me@AhmadAwais.com**. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

## Development Setup

<Accordion title="Setting up the development environment">
  1. **Fork the repository** on [GitHub](https://github.com/ahmadawais/shades-of-purple-vscode)
  2. **Clone your fork** locally
  3. **Open in VS Code** and press `F5` to launch the Extension Development Host
  4. **Make your changes** to the theme files
  5. **Test thoroughly** across different languages and UI elements
  6. **Commit and push** your changes
  7. **Open a pull request** with a clear description of your changes
</Accordion>
